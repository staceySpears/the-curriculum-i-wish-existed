# Solo Weekly Rhythm

A solo learner needs a repeatable rhythm.

Without a rhythm, the work becomes either chaotic or invisible. This weekly structure is a starting point. Adjust it to fit your schedule, but keep the same categories of work.

## Full-time rhythm

A full-time solo learner should aim for five focused workdays.

A useful pattern is:

- three days for new material and exercises
- one day for project work
- one day for review, cleanup, DSA, writing, and self-evaluation

The review day is not optional. It is where hidden confusion becomes visible.

## Part-time rhythm

A part-time solo learner should aim for three to five focused sessions per week.

A useful pattern is:

- two sessions for new material
- one longer session for project work
- one shorter session for review, DSA, or reflection

If you only have one or two sessions in a week, begin the next week by reviewing before moving forward.

## Weekly checklist

At the end of each week, answer:

- What did I complete?
- What did I try that did not work?
- What bug or confusion taught me something?
- What evidence did I produce?
- What should I review before moving on?
- What is the first task for next week?

## Anti-drift rule

If you cannot name what you learned this week, slow down and write it out.

If you cannot name what evidence you produced, you may have consumed material without practicing enough.
