# Solo Accountability Plan

Solo learners need external reality checks.

This does not require a formal mentor, but it does require intentional evidence. The accountability plan helps a solo learner avoid disappearing into private progress that cannot be inspected.

## Minimum accountability system

Keep your work in repositories when possible.

Commit regularly with meaningful messages.

Maintain a learning log.

Complete self-reviews before gates.

Ask for review on major projects.

Compare your work against public examples after you have made your own attempt.

## Review ladder

Use the strongest available review option.

1. Self-review with rubric
2. Recorded walkthrough
3. Peer or community review
4. Mentor or working engineer review
5. Public artifact review, such as an open source PR or project demo

Do not wait for perfect review access. Use the best review you can get.

## Accountability prompts

- What would I show someone to prove progress?
- What would a reviewer probably question?
- What have I avoided because it feels uncomfortable?
- What old work should I revisit?
- What project artifact is becoming portfolio-worthy?

## When you are stuck

Being stuck is not a reason to disappear.

When stuck, write:

- what you expected
- what happened
- what you tried
- what you think might be true
- what question you need answered

Then ask for help with that specific context.
