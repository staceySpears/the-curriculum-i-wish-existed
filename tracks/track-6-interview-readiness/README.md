# Track 6 — Interview Readiness

Track 6 is where the learner becomes legible to the industry.

This track does not teach new programming concepts. It teaches the learner to communicate what they already know: in code, in explanation, in story, and on paper.

## What this track teaches

- DSA review by pattern: sliding window, two pointers, BFS/DFS, dynamic programming basics
- Behavioral story structure using STAR-L
- Technical explanation and project walkthrough
- System design fundamentals: scalability, trade-offs, components
- Portfolio curation and project presentation
- Resume and profile writing
- Mock interview practice with and without AI
- Honest self-evaluation of readiness

## AI policy

AI may be used as a study partner in Track 6.

AI may generate practice prompts, simulate interview questions, critique behavioral stories, help revise resume bullets, ask system design questions, and give feedback on explanations.

Track 6 must also include AI-free practice. The goal is to perform without AI in the room. Use AI to prepare, not to depend on.

See `../../AI_POLICY.md` and `../../spines/ai-collaboration.md`.

## Modules

Modules cover DSA patterns, behavioral story building, technical explanation practice, system design basics, portfolio review, and mock interview structure.

## Exercises

Track 6 exercises are practice sessions: a timed DSA problem, a recorded explanation, a behavioral story draft, a mock system design.

See `exercises/README.md`.

## Project

The Track 6 project is an interview-ready portfolio: curated projects with READMEs, a behavioral story set, and a technical walkthrough of one project.

See `project/README.md`.

## Gate

The Track 6 gate proves that you can explain your work, tell a credible story, and honestly evaluate your own readiness.

See `gate.md`.

## Track 6 definition of done

Track 6 is complete when you can:

- solve and explain a DSA problem out loud without AI
- tell a credible behavioral story with STAR-L structure
- walk through a project you built and explain the decisions
- describe the tradeoffs in your design
- evaluate your own interview readiness honestly
- present a portfolio that makes your work legible to a stranger
- pass the Track 6 gate
