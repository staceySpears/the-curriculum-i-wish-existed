# Track 6 Gate — Interview Readiness

This gate verifies that you are ready to leave Track 6 and begin engineering practice in Track 7.

The goal is not to be perfect. The goal is to be legible, honest, and prepared enough to perform in a real interview without AI assistance.

## Required artifact

Submit your Track 6 interview-ready portfolio.

It should include:

- at least two curated project READMEs, each explaining the problem, approach, tradeoffs, and how to run it
- a behavioral story set with at least three STAR-L stories covering different situations
- a technical walkthrough document for one project
- a self-evaluation of interview readiness with honest gaps identified

## Correctness requirements

You must be able to:

- solve a DSA problem out loud without AI assistance and explain your reasoning
- walk through one of your projects from problem to decision to implementation
- tell a behavioral story cleanly in under three minutes
- answer follow-up questions on your technical decisions without looking at notes

## Explanation requirements

You must explain:

- the key data structure or algorithm in your DSA solution and why you chose it
- a tradeoff you made in one of your projects
- a time you failed, struggled, or changed direction, and what you learned
- what you would do differently if you rebuilt one of your projects today
- where your gaps are and what you are still working on

## Self-evaluation requirement

Write an honest readiness assessment that answers:

- What types of DSA problems feel solid? Which feel fragile?
- Which behavioral stories feel genuine? Which still feel rehearsed?
- What system design concepts do you understand? What is still fuzzy?
- What would a tough interviewer catch you on?

## Spine evidence

### Self-evaluation

Show your readiness assessment. Is it honest? Does it name real gaps, not just generic ones?

### Technical communication

Show one project README and your technical walkthrough. Can a stranger understand your work, your decisions, and how to run it without asking you questions?

### Big-O and data structures

In your DSA practice, explain the complexity of your solution to one problem. What data structure did you choose and why?

### AI collaboration

Describe how you used AI as a study partner during Track 6. What did it help with? What did you practice without it? What would break down if AI were unavailable in the actual interview?

## Outcome

### Pass

You can explain your work, tell a credible story, solve and communicate a DSA problem, and honestly assess your own readiness.

### Revise

You are close, but must strengthen missing evidence: a project README that does not yet stand alone, a behavioral story that is too vague, or a readiness assessment that does not name real gaps.

### Repeat

You should revisit Track 6 if you cannot solve and explain a DSA problem without AI, cannot tell a behavioral story in a coherent structure, or cannot walk through a project you built without reading your own code.
